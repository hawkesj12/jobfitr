jobfitr Experience Design Board · v1 concept

A job search that
feels human.

Job hunting is painful and the tools make it worse. jobfitr turns a short conversation into a ranked shortlist of real, direct-to-company openings — and makes the act of sorting through them feel considered instead of exhausting. Professional, with life. Not a game.

2026-07-13 ● grounded in 4 Arcanum field pages honest engagement, not dark patterns vanilla JS · FastAPI · cached-harvest
The principle

One honest peak beats a hundred cheap thrills

The engagement research is blunt: copying the slot-machine loop wholesale reads as manipulation, and people increasingly resent it. So jobfitr spends its whole delight budget where it counts and stays quiet everywhere else. Every moment carries information — a transition, a value, a real win.

01Peak-end, not sprinkle

People remember an experience by its best moment and its ending. Build one great peak — the page lift and the fit reveal — and a warm close. No confetti on every click.

02Honest open loops

The pull to keep going comes from real roles waiting below, best-fit first. Never manufactured scarcity, fake urgency, or a broken-streak guilt trip.

03Real value on return

The reason to come back is true: the harvest refreshes every few hours, so "14 fresh roles since Tuesday" is a fact, not a trick.

The chat fills the search by talking, the page lifts into results, and a single wide card holds the role in focus while the next-best waits, masked, below it. Each mockup is live — the gauges fill as you scroll.

The thread that runs through it

The assistant never fully leaves — and it's how you steer

The chat stays docked at the top as a control surface. It speaks on real events, not a chatty timer, and it's the one place you change your search — no "reset" button, no "retry." You just talk to it.

◆Speaks on events

A couple of passes in a row: "Nothing landing? Want to widen it?" A strong match surfacing: "This one scores high." Earned lines only — never filler.

◆Refine by talking

"Actually, remote only." The model emits a partial config update, it merges, and the list re-scores. The config is mutable by conversation — intuitive, no controls.

◆Clean by design

Because the chat is the refine surface, the interface stays uncluttered: no retry, no reset, no settings drawer. Going "up to the chat" is the obvious move.

The line we hold

Honest engagement vs. dark pattern

Same mechanics the casinos and dating apps use — pointed at real value instead of against the user. The left column ships. The right column never does.

✓ Fair game — ships
  • One crafted peak — the page lift + the fit reveal, then a warm close.
  • Honest open loop — real roles waiting below, best-fit first.
  • Real return value — "14 fresh since Tuesday," true from the harvest.
  • Real scoreboard — the board counts actual applications.
  • Real token streaming — honest presence, natural cadence.
  • Reduced-motion honored — every moment degrades gracefully.
✕ Dark pattern — never
  • Confetti on everything — reward inflation; trains the eye to ignore it.
  • Manufactured scarcity — fake "3 people viewing this right now."
  • The near-miss — "you were 1 away." A hard red line.
  • Streak guilt — "don't lose your streak" notifications.
  • Slot-tumbler drama — fake suspense on instant content.
  • Faked latency — typing dots as theater. Erodes trust.

The one-line test: does this animation carry information — a transition, a value change, a real achievement — or is it decoration? If it's decoration, cut it.

Under the hood · why it's safe

Two planes, one gate

The AI talks and fills a config. The scoring engine reads a cached snapshot and never touches an external API. The only thing that crosses between them is a validated config — which is both the security boundary and the reason the whole thing stays fast and cheap.

Proven by spike: hostile AI output (DROP TABLE, <script>, rm -rf /, hallucinated keys) collapses into a harmless search config — unknown keys ignored, bad types defaulted, strings treated only as keywords. The attack ceiling is "a weird search."

Needs your call before build

Two decisions

Decision 01

What does the gauge's 100% mean?

Relative: top match this search = 100%, always looks full — but "100%" is just "best of this search."

Absolute: scored against a fixed reference, so a weak match visibly reads weak and a real winner still sweeps near-full.

Recommendation: absolute-hybrid — honest, and still lands the satisfying near-full sweep on a genuine match.

Decision 02

Bless the taste line as the north star

The whole board deliberately rejects the slot-machine kit — tumbler, near-miss, streak-guilt, confetti-on-everything — for one crafted peak and honest loops.

That restraint is what makes it read as a professional product with life, not a game — and it's the stronger portfolio story. Confirm it as the direction.
